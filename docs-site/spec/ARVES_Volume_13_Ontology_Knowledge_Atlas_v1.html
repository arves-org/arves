ARVES Volume-13: Ontology & Knowledge Atlas v1.0

STATUS: KNOWLEDGE CONSTITUTION (FROZEN AFTER APPROVAL)

Part 1 — Purpose

Transform Information into Shared Meaning.

Part 2 — Ontology Philosophy

ARVES is a Meaning-First platform. Ontology is the foundation of intelligence.

Part 3 — Knowledge Model

Knowledge consists of Entities, Relationships and Meaning.

Part 4 — Universal Entity Model

Person, Organization, Team, Agent, Device, Knowledge Object, Conversation, Goal, Task, Project, Strategy, Event and Resource.

Part 5 — Universal Relationship Model

OWNS, MEMBER_OF, WORKS_ON, REPORTS_TO, RELATED_TO, DEPENDS_ON, CREATED_BY, ASSIGNED_TO, LOCATED_IN and PART_OF.

Part 6 — Knowledge Object Model

Fact, Observation, Insight, Hypothesis, Decision and Lesson.

Part 7 — Fact Model

Represents verified truth.

Part 8 — Hypothesis Model

Represents potential explanations and assumptions.

Part 9 — Decision Model

Represents selected courses of action.

Part 10 — Event Model

Represents things that happened and their consequences.

Part 11 — Temporal Model

Valid From, Valid To and Observed At.

Part 12 — Trust Model

Every knowledge item carries a trust score.

Part 13 — Provenance Model

Track source, collector, transformation and timestamps.

Part 14 — Evidence Model

Every knowledge item may be supported by evidence.

Part 15 — Contradiction Model

Manage conflicting facts, sources and conclusions.

Part 16 — Knowledge Graph Model

Knowledge Graph = Nodes + Edges + Semantics.

Part 17 — Semantic Layer

Concepts, taxonomies, categories and meaning definitions.

Part 18 — Domain Ontologies

People, Project, Goal, Finance and other domain-specific ontologies.

Part 19 — Ontology Registry

Store ontology definitions, versions and dependencies.

Part 20 — Canonical Model Registry

Store canonical entity definitions and schemas.

Part 21 — Entity Resolution Model

Transform many representations into one canonical entity.

Part 22 — Knowledge Lifecycle

Created → Validated → Linked → Used → Updated → Archived.

Part 23 — Knowledge Ownership

Owns ontology definitions, knowledge objects, relationships and canonical models.

Part 24 — Knowledge Governance

Manage quality, trust, consistency, lineage and semantic integrity.

Part 25 — Knowledge Success Criteria

Information must be transformed into reusable meaning.

Part 26 — Final Definition

Ontology & Knowledge Atlas = ARVES Universal Meaning System.
