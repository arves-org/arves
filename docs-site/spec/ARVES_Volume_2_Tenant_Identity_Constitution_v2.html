ARVES Volume-2: Tenant & Identity Constitution v2.0

STATUS: FOUNDATIONAL CONSTITUTION (FROZEN AFTER APPROVAL)

Part 1 — Purpose

Resolve ownership, access, authority, governance and isolation across the ARVES platform.

Part 2 — Tenant Philosophy

A Tenant is an Intelligence Boundary, not merely a company. It may represent a person, team, organization, agent network or device network.

Part 3 — Tenant Model

Tenant contains Organizations, Teams, Persons, Agents, Devices, Workspaces, Policies, Resources, Models, Tools, Connectors and Billing.

Part 4 — Entity Constitution

Core entities: Person, Team, Organization, Agent and Device.

Part 5 — Person

Represents human intelligence. Owns identity, preferences, memberships, roles, permissions, memories and goals.

Part 6 — Team

Represents collective intelligence such as engineering, research or product teams.

Part 7 — Organization

Represents institutional intelligence including enterprises, universities and communities.

Part 8 — Agent

Represents an autonomous intelligence unit such as Research Agent, Planning Agent, Knowledge Agent or Execution Agent.

Part 9 — Device

Represents a physical execution endpoint such as laptop, mobile device, robot, IoT device or vehicle.

Part 10 — Workspace Model

Workspace is the primary collaboration boundary inside a tenant.

Part 11 — Space Model

Knowledge Space (Information Core), Cognitive Space (Cognitive Core), Strategic Space (Strategic Core).

Part 12 — Membership Model

Tenant → Organization → Team → Person. Supports multi-membership and cross-workspace participation.

Part 13 — Role Model

Owner, Administrator, Manager, Member, Viewer and Agent.

Part 14 — Permission Model

Read, Write, Execute, Manage and Administer.

Part 15 — Policy Model

Defines who can do what on which resource under which conditions.

Part 16 — Resource Model

Documents, Databases, APIs, Tools, Models, Connectors, Knowledge Graphs, Memories, Goals, Strategies and Workflows.

Part 17 — Ownership Constitution

Every resource, goal, strategy, knowledge object and agent must have a defined owner.

Part 18 — Authentication

Password, SSO, OIDC, OAuth2, API Keys and Service Accounts.

Part 19 — Authorization

RBAC and ABAC combined.

Part 20 — Audit Constitution

Every action records Who, When, What and Why.

Part 21 — Security Principles

Tenant Isolation, Workspace Isolation, Least Privilege, Zero Trust and Defense in Depth.

Part 22 — Identity Contracts

Tenant→Identity, Identity→Information, Identity→Cognitive, Identity→Strategic.

Part 23 — Agent Governance

Every agent has an owner, policies, permissions, capabilities and audit trail.

Part 24 — Device Governance

Every device has identity, trust level, capabilities, owner and policies.

Part 25 — Success Criteria

100% Tenant Isolation, Workspace Isolation, Identity Traceability, Ownership Coverage, Policy Enforcement, Agent Governance and Device Governance.
