ARVES Volume-11: Capability Atlas v1.0

STATUS: CAPABILITY CONSTITUTION (FROZEN AFTER APPROVAL)

Part 1 — Purpose

Define the complete capability map of the ARVES Universal Intelligence Platform.

Part 2 — Capability Philosophy

Capabilities are the functional building blocks of intelligence. Every capability belongs to a core and has a maturity level.

Part 3 — Capability Classification Model

L1 Core Capability, L2 Advanced Capability, L3 Autonomous Capability, L4 AGI-Oriented Capability.

Part 4 — Foundation Capabilities

Identity, Governance, Knowledge, Memory, Reasoning and Decision.

Part 5 — Information Core Capabilities

Provider Registry, Discovery Engine, Schema Intelligence, Entity Resolution, Ontology Registry, Knowledge Graph, Trust Model, Provenance, Metadata Catalog, Knowledge Lifecycle.

Part 6 — Cognitive Core Capabilities

Context Modeling, Intent Understanding, Memory Systems, Reasoning, Risk Intelligence, Confidence Engine, Decision Engine, Reflection, Calibration, Stability Control, Temporal Intelligence, Cognitive Economy.

Part 7 — Strategic Core Capabilities

Goals, Goal Hierarchy, Planning, Task Decomposition, Tradeoff Analysis, Resource Allocation, Opportunity Detection, Scenario Simulation, Priority Arbitration, Compounding Strategy.

Part 8 — Experience Core Capabilities

Workspace, Search, Knowledge Explorer, Graph Explorer, Conversation, Voice, Presence, Continuous Presence, Cross Device, Notifications, Emotional Presence, Social Awareness.

Part 9 — Evolution Core Capabilities

Reflection, Learning, Preference Learning, Memory Consolidation, Meta Learning, Trust Calibration, Knowledge Improvement, Future Prediction, Self Benchmarking, Identity Continuity.

Part 10 — Embodied Core Capabilities

Vision, Sensor Fusion, Localization, Mapping, Navigation, Motion Planning, Robotics, Smart Home Integration, Vehicle Integration, World State Memory.

Part 11 — Runtime Capabilities

Events, Commands, Queries, Orchestration, Workflows, Scheduling, Multi-Agent Runtime, Context Propagation, Policy Enforcement, Observability.

Part 12 — Foundation Capability Levels

Identity, Governance, Knowledge, Memory, Reasoning and Decision are mandatory L1 capabilities.

Part 13 — Advanced Capability Levels

Confidence, Risk, Reflection, Tradeoffs, Simulation, Voice, Notifications and Personalization are L2 capabilities.

Part 14 — Autonomous Capability Levels

Presence, Opportunity Detection, Meta Learning, Agent Collaboration and Robotics are L3 capabilities.

Part 15 — AGI-Oriented Capability Levels

Self Directed Cognition, Autonomous Strategic Evolution, Recursive Self Improvement and General Physical Intelligence are L4 capabilities.

Part 16 — Capability Ownership

Every capability has a single owning core and explicit contracts.

Part 17 — Capability Dependencies

Capabilities may depend on lower-level capabilities but must remain logically separated.

Part 18 — Capability Maturity Model

Emerging, Experimental, Operational, Production and Strategic maturity levels.

Part 19 — Capability Roadmap

Capabilities evolve from L1 through L4 maturity over time.

Part 20 — Capability Observability

Every capability must expose metrics, health signals and operational traces.

Part 21 — Capability Governance

Capabilities are governed through ownership, policies and lifecycle management.

Part 22 — Capability Success Criteria

Capabilities must be measurable, reusable, observable and evolvable.

Part 23 — Final Definition

Capability Atlas = Complete Intelligence Capability Map of ARVES.
