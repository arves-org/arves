ARVES Volume-6: Experience Core Bible v1.0

STATUS: CORE CONSTITUTION (FROZEN AFTER APPROVAL)

Part 1 — Purpose

Transform Direction into Interaction.

Part 2 — Experience Philosophy

Experience combines Knowledge, Thinking, Strategy and Presence into user-facing interaction.

Part 3 — Experience Runtime Model

Observe User → Understand Context → Generate Experience → Interact → Collect Feedback → Adapt.

Part 4 — Workspace Constitution

Workspace is the primary collaboration surface containing knowledge, goals, agents, conversations, dashboards and workflows.

Part 5 — Search Experience

Support Semantic Search, Keyword Search, Graph Search and Hybrid Search.

Part 6 — Knowledge Explorer

Enable navigation and exploration of knowledge assets.

Part 7 — Graph Explorer

Enable exploration of entities, relationships and knowledge networks.

Part 8 — Conversation Constitution

Conversation is the primary cognitive interface supporting chat, context awareness and memory awareness.

Part 9 — Voice Experience

Support speech-to-text, text-to-speech, voice commands and voice conversations.

Part 10 — Presence Constitution

Provide continuous availability, awareness and reachability.

Part 11 — Continuous Presence

Enable always-available intelligence experiences.

Part 12 — Cross Device Experience

Support desktop, mobile, web, voice devices and robots.

Part 13 — Notification Intelligence

Deliver alerts, recommendations, goal updates and risk warnings.

Part 14 — Admin Console

Provide platform administration and operational management.

Part 15 — User Preference Model

Store preferences, interaction styles, language and accessibility settings.

Part 16 — Personalization Engine

Adapt experiences to users, teams and organizations.

Part 17 — Emotional Presence

Analyze sentiment, emotional context and interaction tone.

Part 18 — Social Awareness

Understand relationships, groups, teams and communication patterns.

Part 19 — Experience Memory

Store preferences, interaction history and usage patterns.

Part 20 — Experience Observability

Track usage, engagement, satisfaction and task completion.

Part 21 — Experience Ownership

Owns Workspace, Conversation, Voice, Presence, Notifications and Personalization.

Part 22 — Experience Runtime Flow

Workspace → Conversation → Knowledge Access → Interaction → Feedback → Adaptation.

Part 23 — Experience Contracts

Workspace Contract, Conversation Contract, Presence Contract and Notification Contract.

Part 24 — Success Criteria

Direction must be transformed into natural, continuous and personalized interaction.

Part 25 — Final Definition

Experience Core = ARVES Interaction Engine.
