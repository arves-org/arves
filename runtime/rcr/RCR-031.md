## HONEST LANGUAGE (load-bearing)

- Every "agent" is a **deterministic test actor** — a registered identity
  (RCR-029) driven by scripted, seeded schedules — **NOT an AI model**.
- **"Byzantine-ish" means LAWFUL-API misuse** under the trusted-single-host
  model (design §3.16/OQ-1): adversaries use the public runtime surface
  (including the raw frozen gateway) — never memory corruption, never a
  network adversary (no network exists; in-process `ClusterSim`).
- ENFORCED (structural, proven here): attribution is UNFORGEABLE at the
  content-addressing layer — a replay dedupes to the SAME truth, an address
  rebind to a re-attributed payload is refused (RCR-005), a re-wrap is a
  DIFFERENT truth under the re-wrapper's own Who. NOT ENFORCED (pinned by
  RCR-029/030, not re-claimed): that the CALLER is the agent — the
  worn-registered-identity limit stands (v2.0 debt #8 / design OQ-1).
- Order-independence is a **SET-level** claim (truth set + attribution-trail
  set identical across all schedule permutations); the shard log ORDER
  lawfully differs per schedule (IDR-005 total order) and the proof asserts
  that difference so the claim is non-vacuous.

## Executable proofs (all deterministic; `tests/multi_agent_adversarial.rs`)

| Mandate item | Test |
|---|---|
| **(a) Agent storms — the order-independence proof.** 3 agents × 4 proposals + 3 injected duplicates = 15 submissions, run under SEVEN schedules (identity, reverse, five seeded xorshift permutations): per schedule everything flows through the I4 scheduler into the shard leader's frozen gateway; the FINAL TRUTH SET (content id → payload) and the attribution-trail SET are IDENTICAL across ALL schedules on EVERY replica; duplicates collapse VISIBLY at the ledger (3 `Deduplicated`) and never fork (15 truths: 3 registrations + 12 effects, each distinct proposal computed once); HONESTY PIN: at least one permutation lands different shard-state BYTES than the identity schedule (log order differs — the set claim is proven over genuinely different interleavings); the identical seeded schedule re-run is byte-identical state | `agent_storm_truth_set_identical_across_all_seeded_schedule_permutations` |
| **(b) Lawful-API misuse — attribution unforgeable, floods never double-commit.** REPLAY: bob re-commits alice's exact envelope through the raw gateway → dedupes to alice's ORIGINAL truth, zero new truth, attribution still alice; REBIND: bob proposes a payload re-attributing alice's effect to himself under alice's ORIGINAL content address → refused `ContentIntegrity` (RCR-005 — a committed attribution can never be rewritten); RE-WRAP: bob wraps alice's effect bytes under his own identity → a DIFFERENT fresh truth attributed to BOB, alice's row untouched, the two Whos never blur on any replica; FLOOD: the same proposal 4× through each of TWO racing schedulers + two direct re-proposals → exactly ONE fresh commit ever, exactly ONE trail row, on every replica (at-least-once compute, at-most-once truth — ORCH-004) | `replay_rebind_and_rewrap_cannot_forge_attribution_and_floods_never_double_commit` |
| **(c) Partition during multi-agent work.** The shard leader is isolated into a minority of one mid-run: the minority-side agent proposal FAILS HONESTLY (`NotReplicated`/`NotLeader` — CP: no quorum ⇒ no truth; committed_count unchanged, no partial truth — IDR-004); the majority elects a successor and multi-agent work CONTINUES (attributed effect + a full decision-flow commit through the new leader); heal ⇒ every replica byte-identical; every ACKED attributed truth survives on all replicas; the refused proposal is cleanly retriable and lands EXACTLY ONCE (ORCH-004) | `partition_minority_proposals_fail_honestly_and_heal_loses_no_attributed_truth` |
| **(d) Full-cluster deterministic replay INCLUDING attribution (ORCH-003).** A two-tenant multi-agent history (3 registrations, scheduler-borne + direct attributed effects incl. a collapsed duplicate, policy + self-then-peer approval + Blocked/admitted decisions, a scripted conflict race with its compliance events) is committed; EVERY node crash-recovers from its own durable WAL: byte-identical per-shard truth AND an identical attribution trail (who/what/offset) AND identical derived decisions per subject AND the identical compliance ledger, on every node; the two tenants' trails never blur before or after replay (SHARD-001) | `full_cluster_replay_from_wal_rebuilds_identical_truth_and_attribution_trail` |
| **(e) The multi-agent Scenario axis, live.** Axis 9 instantiated with `Verdict::Pass`; every required invariant (ORCH-001..004, OWN-001, SHARD-001) and property (isolation, policy-gates-fired, safety-gates-blocked, replay) derived Held from behaviour; the §5.3 multi-agent fields populated and asserted (2 policy-gate records Held, 1 first-committed-wins arbitration record); first LCW node evidence; honest fingerprint asserted | `arves-conformance::live::tests::live_multi_agent_coordination_scenario_passes` |

## Design resolutions (simplest option consistent with IDR-001..005 — recorded, never silently invented)

| DR-2 | **Seeded permutations = deterministic xorshift64 Fisher–Yates** with recorded seeds (identity + reverse + 5 fixed seeds) — scripted, replayable schedules per the mandate; zero OS randomness. |

## I5 MILESTONE SUMMARY — what I5 delivers (RCR-029 · RCR-030 · RCR-031)

**Delivered, executable, on the frozen v1.0 contracts (all additive, v1.1-class):**

1. **Agent identity as committed truth** (RCR-029): versioned, governed
   (owner-mandatory), content-addressed, shard-bound-for-life identities
   registered through the one frozen gateway; addressable from every replica;
   recover with the truth base (design OQ-2 resolved truth-side).
2. **The LCW shared-truth surface** (RCR-029): the first `WorkingMemory`/
   `LiveWorkspace` implementations + `WorldView` — a read-only, versioned,
   coherent per-shard fold of committed truth (the declared read basis of
   every multi-agent gate), derived and disposable.
3. **Attribution** (RCR-029): the Who rides INSIDE the committed payload — on
   every replica, in the WAL, tamper-evident (RCR-002), and — proven this
   stage — unforgeable at the content-addressing layer (replay/rebind/re-wrap).
4. **Multi-agent orchestration over ONE shared truth base** (RCR-030):
   scheduler-borne attributed proposals (agents never commit — ORCH-001);
   the decision/compliance truth flow (policy truths, separate approval
   truths with proposer ≠ approver, refusals COMMITTED as compliance truth);
   deterministic first-committed-wins conflict resolution with the loser
   receiving the winner (the G1 enterprise-os semantics at runtime grade).
5. **Adversarial closure + the axis-9 conformance scenario** (RCR-031):
   storm order-independence, misuse refusals, partition honesty, full-cluster
   replay including attribution, the live `multi-agent-coordination-
   distributed` artifact (`Verdict::Pass`) with the first populated
   multi-agent artifact fields and first LCW node evidence.

**HONEST SCOPE — what I5 is NOT (nothing beyond this is claimed):**
Agents are deterministic test actors, NOT AI models — no LLM, no reasoning
engine, no autonomy. The vehicle is the in-process `ClusterSim` — no network
transport, no network fault-tolerance claim. Identity is structural, not
cryptographic (v2.0 debt #8 / OQ-1; worn-identity + policy-bypass pins stand,
RCR-029 A2 / RCR-030 A1/A2). The frozen `Orchestrator` plan-graph contract
REMAINS contract-only: delegation, coordination patterns, arbitration-policy
language (OQ-8), plan-graph execution are NOT built. No leader-side admission
re-check (OQ-3 IDR pending — must include the policy re-check, RCR-030 E3).
No revocation of identities/policies/approvals. No HITL sequencing (approvals
are truths, not workflow steps). No durable decision-trace emission (OQ-10).
No cross-shard flows/sagas. No `uci.*` ontology registration (O-006 CCP
pending). LCW-001, G-001, QUERY-001, CAP-*, ENG-* remain PROPOSED (CCP-GATE
required) — cited as intent only, never enforced. Conformance is
L3(scoped)-style honest labeling — never blanket L4 (the SCF L4 ladder
presupposes evidence grades this in-process vehicle cannot mint).

**What I6 (Reference Products) inherits from I5:**
the multi-agent runtime surface as a frozen-contract customer API (identity
registration · attributed proposals · the decision/compliance flow · the
`WorldView` read basis) — plus the recorded pending instruments: the OQ-3
leader-side admission IDR (with policy re-check), the O-006 decision/
compliance ontology CCP, the OQ-8 arbitration-policy instrument, the
Orchestrator plan-graph/delegation stages, IDR-007 placement, bridge exposure
of the multi-agent flow to non-Rust products, Kernel batch-commit (v1.1 #3)
for hot shards (OQ-5), and the v2.0 authenticated-commit RCR (#8) before any
untrusted-host deployment.
