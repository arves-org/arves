## Executable proofs (all deterministic; `tests/adversarial_scheduling.rs`)

| Mandate item | Test |
|---|---|
| **(a) Storm / duplicate / reorder schedules** — 4 unique invocations submitted 11× in two ORDERINGS by two RACING schedulers: every duplicate collapses VISIBLY at the ledger (6+1 `Deduplicated` decisions, exact counts), compute is honestly at-least-once (8 executions = the recorded `Computed` decisions), and across BOTH decision logs each unique key lands as FRESH truth EXACTLY once — 4 commits, byte-identical replicas, regardless of storm order (ORCH-004 at the scheduling layer) | `storm_duplicate_and_reordered_schedules_never_double_execute_orch004` |
| **(b) Node death mid-invocation** — the placed leader dies between placement and quorum: retriable verdict, re-queue under the SAME key (IDR-004); survivors elect; the retry replays FROM THE RECORD (engine run count stays 1 — ORCH-003) and commits through the successor; queued work behind it gets a VISIBLE fresh placement on the successor; then the SCHEDULER dies too — a fresh scheduler re-derives the IDENTICAL content-addressed key from the plan alone, recomputes (lawful at-least-once) and BOTH re-proposed effects resolve `deduped: true` (zero fresh commits — no duplicate). Epilogue: the dead node rejoins and converges byte-identically | `node_death_mid_invocation_replaces_and_never_duplicates_commit` |
| **(c) Backpressure honesty** — the ACCOUNTING EQUATION: 12 submit calls ≡ 6 `Admitted` + 6 `AdmissionDenied` decisions logged 1:1 with the outcomes returned (no fifth, silent path); a denial leaves NO ledger half-state (stateless ⇒ retriable, unlike quarantine); refused work re-submitted after drain admits and completes; 6 distinct keys, 6 executions, 6 truths on every replica — a silent drop is impossible by accounting | `overcapacity_refusals_are_explicit_accounted_and_retriable_never_silent` |
| **(d) Failure isolation** — a POISON STORM (3 distinct invocations of a falsely-`Deterministic` capability interleaved AHEAD of healthy work) quarantines terminally (probe double-invoke only: 6 runs, zero retries) while every healthy invocation in the same shard AND the other tenant completes within a bounded tick budget; re-submitted poison collapses onto its quarantine WITHOUT re-invoking; NEW work in both shards still completes afterwards; the poison contributed ZERO truth (7 healthy truths, byte-identical everywhere) | `poison_capability_storm_cannot_block_shard_or_cluster` |
| **(e) Leadership change mid-schedule** — the old leader survives, steps down and REJOINS as a follower; an invocation committed under the old regime is raced by a second scheduler under the new leader: its re-commit resolves `deduped: true` to the old-era truth (zero fresh commits — exactly-once across the regime change); NEW work follows the new leader; across BOTH schedulers the fresh-commit key set is exactly {X, Y}, one each; the rejoined old leader holds the identical truth bytes | `leadership_change_mid_schedule_lands_each_invocation_exactly_once` |
| **(f) Conformance extension** — the live `capability-scheduling-distributed` artifact: 3 node probes, every required invariant (ORCH-001/002/003/004, OWN-001, SHARD-001) and property (TenantWorkspaceIsolation, SafetyGatesBlockedUnsafePlans, PolicyGatesFired, ReplayReproducesTrace) derived **Held from live behaviour**, `Verdict::Pass`; honest fingerprint asserts "no network transport" AND "pending IDR-007" | `arves-conformance::live::tests::live_capability_scheduling_scenario_passes` |
| **(g) Policy-flip re-submission (adversarial revision)** — the Governance `PolicyVerdict` is a caller input NOT encoded in the ORCH-004 key: a Deny-quarantined key re-submitted under `Allow` collapses onto the recorded refusal for the scheduler's lifetime (visible `Deduplicated` + inspectable `state_of`, engine never invoked, zero truth); a FRESH scheduler re-admits the identical key, executes once and lands the truth byte-identically (ORCH-002 discardability is the flip path) | `policy_flip_resubmission_collapses_in_scheduler_lifetime_fresh_scheduler_readmits` |

| DR-3 | **What "node death mid-invocation" means in-process:** the sim cannot kill a thread mid-`dispatch_one`, so death is modeled as the strongest observable equivalent — the placed node is isolated BETWEEN the placement decision and quorum (stale AP presence has not noticed: the honesty of RCR-027 DR-10), then reported down. Scheduler death = `drop` + rebuild-from-plan. Both recorded honestly; no claim of OS-process kill or network death is made. |
| DR-4 | **Axis 8 instantiated via its backpressure/tenant-isolation clause only** (the same scoping RCR-025 DR-3 recorded for I3): the in-process harness exercises NO volume, throughput or latency — no performance claim is made or implied (design §3.15: budgets belong to IDR-007). |

The I5 Orchestrator (plan-graph execution) remains contract-only and is NOT pre-certified by any of these rows. |
| DR-6 | **Policy-flip collapse pinned as-is (adversarial revision):** the Governance `PolicyVerdict` is a caller input to the gate, NOT part of the ORCH-004 dedupe key (which is pure content: shard + capability + manifest identity + canonical input). Consequence, stated precisely: a `Deny` gate-denial quarantines `retriable: false`, and a later re-submission of the same (shard, capability, input) with `Allow` DEDUPLICATES onto the stale refusal for that scheduler's lifetime. No registered invariant breaks (the refusal is visible via `state_of`; the schedule is discardable per ORCH-002, and a fresh scheduler re-admits — that is the flip path). The alternative — re-admitting a `PolicyBlocked` quarantine when the supplied verdict differs — is a placement/admission POLICY choice and is deferred to the IDR-007 instrument, not silently invented here. Behavior pinned by proof (g); the `WorkState::Quarantined` doc now states the verdict-not-in-key precision. |
| DR-7 | **No decision-less queue removal (adversarial revision):** the defensive dispatch arm for a popped item whose ledger state is terminal (unreachable via the public API — `submit` is the only enqueue path and records `Queued`; terminal states are never re-queued) now fails LOUDLY (`unreachable!`) instead of silently returning: if it were ever reached it would be exactly the silent-drop class the backpressure accounting proof (c) declares impossible, so the accounting equation is enforced structurally rather than assumed. |

## I4 MILESTONE SUMMARY (Stages 1–3 = RCR-026, RCR-027, RCR-028)

**What I4 delivers** — cluster-wide capability scheduling on the frozen contracts,
proven end-to-end in-process:

1. **Fabric core (RCR-026):** binding lifecycle with append-only supersession +
   tombstone revocation, authoritative/pinned resolution, and the authorization gate
   (F-UNBOUND hard deny · engine-identity match · capabilities-required · policy
   enforced-not-owned · EffectClass validation) wired through RCR-012 `invoke_enforced`.
2. **Cluster scheduler (RCR-027):** deterministic placement (leader-affine commits /
   compute-anywhere `Pure` — reference policy, IDR-007-pending), per-shard bounded
   admission, quarantine-based failure isolation, shard-partitioned +
   capability-qualified ORCH-004 dedupe, replay-from-record retries, and the full chain
   scheduled-invocation → gate → engine → proposed effects → shard-leader commit →
   replicated truth.
3. **Adversarial + conformance closure (RCR-028):** schedule storms/reorders, node and
   scheduler death, backpressure accounting, poison storms, leadership changes — all
   with exactly-once TRUTH; plus the live `capability-scheduling-distributed`
   conformance artifact (`Verdict::Pass`) and PropertyCheck coverage 7/7 with
   scheduling-layer citations.

**Design §5.4 success criteria, honestly stated:** Architecture PASS (gate green over
9 new downward edges across the milestone; no new layer, no new crate). Conformance:
the §5.1 axes are instantiated **at the design's stated scope** — L3 is claimed only as
"L1/L2 semantics preserved under distributed deployment, in-process simulation"
(the same scoped-L3 language as RCR-022/025), never blanket L3. Invariant coverage:
all 7 registered invariants have named, biting, executable tests including the
scheduling surface. Replay PASS (trace-preferring retries; zero re-invocation within
recorded state). Distributed tests PASS (failover, duplicate dispatch, flood isolation
— in-process). Freeze: no frozen byte changed; every claim traceable to the design.

**HONEST SCOPE — what I4 is NOT (nothing beyond this is claimed):** no network/remote
execution (in-process `ClusterSim`; placement is a recorded node label); no
exactly-once COMPUTE (at-least-once compute / at-most-once truth, proven honestly);
no normative placement/backpressure policy (IDR-007 instrument still required — R8);
no plan-DAG ordering/arbitration/dynamic expansion (Orchestrator = I5); no
sagas/cross-shard flows (OQ-4); no HITL/approval sequencing (`ApprovalRequired`
blocks); no distributed cancellation or timeout enforcement (OQ-6; the manifest
subset carries no Timeout field — recorded debt); no durable decision-trace emission
(OQ-10 — the decision log is in-memory observability); no Failure-Policy
degrade/escalate (deny is the only admission response); no cluster batch commit
(v1.1 debt #3); no authN/authZ (v1.0 trusted single host); external-effect
idempotency stays a provider declaration (v1.1 debt #2); the RCR-012 determinism
probe remains a probe. **CAP-001..009 remain PROPOSED** (CCP-GATE required) — nothing
in I4 enforces them; registered bases used throughout.

**What I5 (Multi-Agent Runtime) inherits from I4:**

- the Orchestrator plan-graph contract (frozen, still contract-only) — DAG-edge
  ordering, arbitration, dynamic expansion and its OWN ORCH-001/002 proof obligations
  (the PropertyCheck rows are explicitly scoped to the I4 scheduling surface);
- HITL/approval sequencing (today `ApprovalRequired` blocks — RCR-026 DR-5);
- durable decision-trace emission + granularity (OQ-10 → IDR-007 / v1.1 debt #3);
- saga/cross-shard flow mechanics (OQ-4 → IDR-007);
- distributed cancellation (OQ-6; CAP-008 PROPOSED) and manifest
  Timeout/Failure-Policy fields (recorded manifest debt);
- IDR-007 "Capability Placement & Backpressure" ratification of the reference
  placement/admission policy (design §6.2), plus the CAP-00n CCP-GATE sponsorship
  question (OQ-5) — INCLUDING the DR-6 question of whether a `PolicyBlocked`
  quarantine should re-admit within one scheduler's lifetime when the supplied
  verdict differs (today: collapse pinned; fresh scheduler is the flip path);
- bridge unification onto the fabric gate (RCR-026 DR-7 follow-up).
