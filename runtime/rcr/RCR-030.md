## HONEST LANGUAGE (load-bearing)

- The "agents" of this stage are **deterministic test actors, NOT AI models**:
  registered identities (RCR-029) driven by scripted, seeded schedules.
- ENFORCED (structural): agents never touch `Kernel::commit` — proposals flow
  through the I4 scheduler's leader-routed commit or the flow's caller-passed
  gateway handle (ORCH-001); every gate reads COMMITTED truth at a DECLARED
  versioned basis (the runtime-grade elevation of G1's in-process maps);
  approvals are separate committed truths with proposer ≠ approver; one
  decision truth derives per subject, deterministically, on every replica.
- NOT ENFORCED (pinned by test, never hidden): that the CALLER is the agent —
  any in-process caller can wear any registered identity (v2.0 debt #8 /
  design OQ-1), and the same raw-gateway path bypasses the POLICY/approval
  gate entirely — a worn registered-Who decision on a policy-scoped subject
  derives with NO approval and unverifiable cites (derivation checks
  registration only, never cites/policy; amendment A2); a policy committed
  after a caller's declared basis does not retro-block that caller (OQ-3
  class, recorded debt); readers of compliance events report the CLAIMED Who
  (RCR-029 A2 discipline).

## Executable proofs (all deterministic; `tests/multi_agent_orchestration.rs`)

| Mandate item | Test |
|---|---|

| **(b) Convergence at every grade** — a duplicate proposal collapses VISIBLY at the scheduler ledger (actor not re-invoked); the same proposal raced through TWO schedulers lands exactly ONE fresh commit; two DIFFERENT agents proposing the same decision content converge to ONE derived decision truth both at the pre-check (nothing committed) and when raced commits land (the losing record is committed trace but never derives; `superseded_attempt` reported honestly); no compliance event for agreement | `duplicate_and_agreeing_proposals_converge_to_one_truth_orch004_across_agents` |
| **(b) FIRST-COMMITTED-WINS** — the scripted TOCTOU race (both agents pass the pre-check on one stale basis, both commit): shard log order decides; the loser receives the WINNER's identity + a committed conflict event citing it; a fresh-basis conflicting proposal is refused PRE-commit (exactly one compliance event, never a decision record); derivation identical on every replica (winner + superseded attempt traced, ONE derives); the MIRRORED schedule flips the winner (the rule is order, not identity); the same schedule twice ⇒ byte-identical shard state | `conflicting_decisions_resolve_first_committed_wins_with_loser_receiving_winner` |
| **(d) Policy/approval flow** — the policy gate reads COMMITTED policy truth; no approval ⇒ Blocked with the refusal COMMITTED as compliance truth citing the policy id; SELF-approval never satisfies (proposer ≠ approver) and the identical repeated refusal CONVERGES onto the same recorded compliance truth (ORCH-004 on the audit ledger); an unregistered approver is refused committing nothing; a registered second agent's approval — a SEPARATE committed truth — admits the decision, which CITES exactly the qualifying approval (never the self-approval), the citation provably PRECEDING the decision in the one committed history; HONEST-LIMIT PIN: a pre-policy declared basis does not see the new policy (OQ-3 class, recorded debt), a fresh basis enforces it | `policy_gate_reads_committed_policy_truth_and_approvals_are_separate_truths` |

## Design resolutions (simplest option consistent with IDR-001..005 — recorded, never silently invented)

| # | Resolution |
|---|---|
| DR-1 | **Code home = `arves-control-plane` (design Q1)**: multi-agent coordination is a Control Plane concern (Vol 9 Part 2); the flow DECIDES (gate, converge, conflict) and the Kernel COMMITS (ORCH-001). No new crate, no new layer (Rule 3); the frozen `Orchestrator` contract stays contract-only. |
| DR-2 | **OQ-3 resolved for this stage CONTROL-PLANE-SIDE, post-commit**: the serialization-point re-check runs as (i) a pure derivation rule — `decision_of` = FIRST governed decision on the subject in shard log order (total per shard, IDR-001/IDR-005; identical on every replica, ORCH-003) — plus (ii) a post-commit reconciliation in `propose_decision` against the at-head world, so a raced loser is detected and reported with the winner's identity + a committed conflict event. The Kernel decides NOTHING (Layer Matrix Kernel "Cannot: orchestrate, plan or execute"); no kernel-side gate is added. The losing record remains in the WAL as honest, append-only, replayable trace (design §3.19) — it never derives, so no second truth exists. The full OQ-3 instrument (leader-side admission refusing the losing record BEFORE it enters the log) stays a recorded later-stage IDR obligation — and that IDR MUST include the POLICY re-check at admission, not just the conflict rule (DR-7 is its trigger): design §3.9's "policy race → serialization-point re-check" row is delivered here for the CONFLICT rule only, a knowingly narrowed, recorded debt. |
| DR-3 | **First-committed-wins with the loser receiving the winner's TruthRef** — the design's own example rule and the G1 `proposeDecision` semantics (`prior.id` cited in the conflict compliance event), elevated: "prior" is now defined by committed log order, not an in-process map. Agreement (same action) CONVERGES with no compliance event — mandate (b)'s "same content converges to ONE truth" at the decision level; the literal same-envelope duplicate converges at the Kernel (ORCH-004), both proven. |
| DR-4 | **The I3 ladder is exercised TEST-SIDE (dev-dep arves-query), not as a src edge**: the flow's read surface is the LCW `WorldView` (the declared-basis discipline of RCR-029 DR-9); agents' external reads go through Query per the design, and the (c) proofs bind the two — a `Linearizable` read's `observed_at` is a valid basis version and the ladder's labels are asserted per tier. A src edge control-plane→query would also be downward (90>60) but adds coupling the flow does not need (Rule 9). |
| DR-5 | **Flow encodings are runtime-internal reference encodings** under the existing `COMMIT_CONTENT` ACS-001 tag (RCR-029 DR-7 pattern): `ARVES.FLOW.{POLICY,APPROVAL,DECISION,COMPLIANCE}.v1` magics, length-prefixed LE parts, trailing-garbage refusal. They are NOT registered `uci.*` ontology types — the O-006 CCP (design §6.2) is the instrument for that and is not pre-empted. |
| DR-6 | **Stage-2 policy semantic = the minimal G1 `checkPolicy` subset**: a committed policy `{name, scope, version}` requires, for any decision whose subject has `scope` as prefix, a separate committed approval truth for that exact subject by a REGISTERED agent ≠ the proposer. One qualifying approval satisfies all applicable policies (they all demand the same evidence). The arbitration/policy LANGUAGE is design OQ-8 (CCP/IDR) — deliberately not invented here. |
| DR-7 | **Policy gate basis = the caller's DECLARED `WorldView`** (RCR-029 DR-9): reads committed truth at an explicit version. Consequence, pinned by test as an honest limit: a policy committed after the declared basis does not retro-block that proposal (same OQ-3 TOCTOU class as DR-2; the conflict rule — which is what makes one-truth-per-subject hold — IS reconciled at the serialization point; the policy re-check is not, and says so). Gate ordering, deliberate: the policy gate (flow step 2) runs BEFORE the convergence pre-check (step 3), so a proposal that AGREES with the already-derived decision but lacks approval yields `Blocked` plus a committed compliance event per DISTINCT proposer — never `Converged`. Deterministic; identical retries converge onto one event (ORCH-004, E2); the per-proposer audit-ledger noise on settled subjects is accepted, to be revisited only if the ledger noise matters (a later stage may check convergence-with-same-action first). |
| DR-8 | **Reconciliation arbiter must contain the flow's own commit** (`ReconcileBehind` otherwise, loudly): a behind store could crown the wrong winner, so it is refused rather than guessed over (lossless-or-loud). The committed record is not rolled back; re-proposal against a caught-up store converges idempotently (proven). In the cluster tests the arbiter is the leader's store — any caught-up replica works. |
| DR-9 | **Decision cites = the QUALIFYING approvals only** (exact subject, registered approver, approver ≠ proposer), sorted: the audit Why cites the evidence that actually authorized the decision — never a self-approval, never noise. Full read-set citation is design OQ-4 and not claimed. |
| DR-10 | **Scheduler-borne agent proposals ride the RCR-029 attribution envelope as the invocation INPUT**, echoed verbatim into the proposed effect by the deterministic actor engine: the committed truth carries the Who inside the addressed content, the fabric-derived ORCH-004 key dedupes per agent+effect+capability+shard, and the Governance `PolicyVerdict` input of the I4 gate is passed through unchanged (enforced-not-owned; distinct from the flow's committed-policy gate, said explicitly). |

## Amendments & errata (adversarial revision, 2026-07-06 — findings applied, never softened)

| # | Severity | Finding → correction |
|---|---|---|
| **A1** | major | **Ungoverned-decision derivation hole CONFIRMED and CLOSED (the RCR-029-A1 analog at the decision surface).** The frozen gateway has no principal and does not verify `content == ACS-hash(payload)`, so any caller can lawfully commit a hand-crafted `AgentDecisionRecord` wearing an arbitrary (unregistered) Who into the shard WAL — and the original `decision_of`, which derived from ANY decoded decision record, would have let an ungoverned record win/block a subject, falsifying "no ungoverned agents" (Vol 2 Part 23) and the flow's registered-proposer gate. Fix: `decisions_on`/`decision_of` now derive GOVERNED records only — the claimed agent must be registered committed truth in the SAME world. Pinned by `smuggled_ungoverned_decision_never_derives` (the smuggle stays visible raw trace; a later governed decision derives over it). HONEST LIMIT kept loud in the same test: a smuggled record wearing a REGISTERED identity still derives — v1.x structural identity (debt #8/OQ-1). |
| **E2** | minor | **Duplicate refusals converge — claim corrected in the proofs.** The first test draft asserted two separate committed compliance events for two identical blocked attempts; the Kernel correctly CONVERGED them (identical canonical payload ⇒ same content id, ORCH-004 applies to the audit ledger too). The test now pins the convergence explicitly (both `Blocked` outcomes return the SAME compliance `TruthRef`; the ledger holds ONE recorded refusal). Behaviour was right; the expectation was wrong. |
| **A2** | minor | **Policy-bypass consequence of the worn identity now pinned executable, not implicit.** The A1 honest-limit pin covered the identity half only; the same raw-gateway path also bypasses the POLICY/approval gate entirely. `smuggled_ungoverned_decision_never_derives` now additionally proves: with a committed policy over the scope (which the governed flow enforces — a flow proposal is `Blocked`), a hand-crafted decision wearing a REGISTERED Who on a policy-scoped subject derives with ZERO approval truths in existence and with unverifiable cites reported verbatim (derivation checks registration only, never cites/policy). Same v1.x debt-#8 class, kept as loud as the identity half; only `propose_decision` is governed-gated. |
| **E3** | minor | **Future OQ-3 IDR obligation widened in DR-2.** Design §3.9's "policy race → §3.8(5) serialization-point re-check" row is delivered for the CONFLICT rule only; the policy gate is never re-evaluated at the serialization point (recorded in DR-2/DR-7 + the honest-limit test pin). Recorded consequence: the pending leader-side admission-re-check IDR MUST include the POLICY re-check, not just the conflict rule, with DR-7 as its trigger. No code change; an accepted, well-documented narrowing, now explicit. |
| **E4** | minor | **Policy-before-convergence gate ordering recorded as deliberate in DR-7.** An agreeing-but-unapproved proposal yields `Blocked` + a committed compliance event per distinct proposer instead of `Converged`; deterministic and ORCH-004-converged for identical retries; per-proposer ledger noise on settled subjects accepted, with the possible later-stage reordering (convergence-with-same-action before the policy gate) noted, not performed. |

## HONEST SCOPE — what Stage 2 is NOT (nothing beyond this is claimed)

No leader-side admission re-check (OQ-3 IDR pending; DR-2/DR-7 record exactly
what is reconciled and what is not). No cryptographic identity (v2.0 debt #8;
worn-identity derivation pinned). No delegation, coordination patterns,
arbitration policies, plan-graph execution (the frozen `Orchestrator`
contract stays contract-only — later I5 stages). No HITL/approval SEQUENCING
(approvals are truths, not workflow steps). No revocation of policies/
approvals/identities (immutable truths in v1.x). No `uci.*` ontology
registration (O-006 CCP pending). No network (in-process `ClusterSim`); no
durable decision-trace emission (OQ-10); no cross-shard flows/sagas. The
compliance READERS report claimed Whos (RCR-029 A2 discipline) — only
DERIVATION is governed-gated (A1), and governed-gated means REGISTRATION
only: the raw-gateway path bypasses the policy/approval gate, so a smuggled
registered-Who decision on a policy-scoped subject derives without any
approval and with unverifiable cites (pinned, amendment A2). LCW-001, G-001, QUERY-001, CAP-*, ENG-*
remain PROPOSED (CCP-GATE required) — cited as intent only, never enforced.
PropertyCheck coverage stays 7 proven / 0 pending (no flip; I5
citation-widening deferred to the milestone's closing stage, RCR-028 DR-5
precedent).

## Invariants (registered only)

- **OWN-001** — no new state owner: policies/approvals/decisions/compliance
  events are Kernel truths; the module is stateless functions; the scheduler
  stays the only (ephemeral) plan holder (drop-invariance re-proven).
- **LAYER-001** — ONE new edge, downward, rank-checked BEFORE adding:
  control-plane(90) → persistence(20). arves-query deliberately dev-dep only
  (DR-4). Architecture gate green over the workspace.
- **SHARD-001** — every flow commit targets the basis world's shard; worlds
  are per-shard folds; the I4 ledger stays shard-partitioned under
  multi-agent storms (re-proven in the interleaving test).
- **ORCH-001** — agents and the Control Plane commit nothing: all truth
  enters through the frozen gateway (scheduler-routed or caller-passed);
  every refusal path proven to commit exactly zero (or exactly the
  compliance event it claims).
- **ORCH-002** — the schedule is discardable (rebuild converges, zero fresh
  commits); the flow holds no state between calls; a policy flip of basis is
  a new world, never cached orchestrator state.
- **ORCH-003** — derivation is a pure fold of the committed trace: identical
  across re-reads, replicas, and re-runs of the same scripted schedule
  (byte-identical shard state pinned twice).
- **ORCH-004** — convergence proven at four grades: scheduler ledger dedupe,
  Kernel dedupe across racing schedulers, decision-level agreement
  convergence, and audit-ledger refusal convergence (E2).
